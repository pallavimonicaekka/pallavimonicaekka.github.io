Understanding the influences of Christianity in the villages of Katkahi parish

Objective

The aim is to explore and collect data and information relevant to the tribal population and Christianity in Jharkhand and observe the trends effects of adoption of Christianity in contemporary times, if any, in terms of population and other basic facilities.

Solution

The contribution of the project includes the collection of data, converting the data from manual registers into the data format, analyzing it and representing the insights through visualizations.

The project is deployed as a narrative in the form of a static newspaper article .

Collection of data

The parish that was visited for data collection was ‘Katkahi’ which falls under the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Ranchi. The church is about 135 km away from the capital of the state, Ranchi. The parish roughly has 57 villages. I was provided access to the baptismal records of the parishioners. The records were maintained in physical registers which contained their names, birth year, the year of baptism, village name. All the records were maintained according to the lineage.

The church records seemed promising as it could help us see the influence of an external phenomenon in the tribal community at the village level. Also, the data hadn’t been worked upon previously and choosing this data would lead to the preservation of the data as well as extract insights from the same. Henceforth, this database along with the contemporary data available was decided for further analysis.

Observations and visualizations

#1 Number of baptism and population of villages

click on image for responsive visualisation

The visualization is a heat map with the year on the x-axis and the villages placed in the increasing order of their population in the y-axis.

Two specific time periods can be seen wherein the number of baptism increases. One happens approximately between 1925-1930 and the second happens between 1950-1955.

  1. The first peak happens for the villages which have a comparatively larger population and then the second peak occurs for the smaller population.
  2. It was the smaller villages wherein the initial conversions took place.

#2 Number of baptism and population of villages

click on image for responsive visualisation

The line graph indicates the number of baptisms over the years in the whole parish along with the events, shown when hovered upon.

  1. Information about the events at the peaks could not be discovered during the research. The events collected occur approximately around 25 years before the peak of 1928.

#3 Number of schools in each village

The outer bars on the semi-circle indicate the number of baptisms with the circles indicating the number of schools in each village. The inner circle indicates the population of each village in 2011.

  1. The village with the most number of baptisms does not have a single school according to the census data of 2011.

#4 Establishment of schools and baptisms over the years

The visualization has the number of baptisms on the left side and the number of schools on the right side.

  1. The peaks for baptism and also the establishment of schools somewhat coincide.
