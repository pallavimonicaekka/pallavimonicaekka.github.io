UX Research: Why don't people cycle?

Project overview

Urban travel is one of the biggest concerns in cities and it mainly revolves around environmental factors and sustainability. Increase in the use of light weight vehicles does not only increase air pollution but also makes traveling on the roads time-consuming and inconvenient for the travelers along with the additional cost of the refueling and maintenance of the vehicle. Bicycles seem to be a cheaper and viable alternative to light weight vehicle and two-wheelers when it comes to covering short distances. Bicycles are cheap, Eco-friendly, healthy, faster in traffic and enjoyable when it comes to exploring places. Despite obvious benefits, seldom people prefer cycling. The objective was to identify the reasons for the same.

Research goals

  1. Observe and understand bicycle environments/ institutions across globe
  2. Notable behaviours, preferences, barriers and technologies that impact usage of the bicycle
  3. Identify needs and opportunities to inform the product design strategy

Methodology

Where and when will the research be carried out?

The research was carried out in Mumbai (urban) during summer of 2017

What kind of Data needs to be collected?

Data about the behaviour, motivations and concerns of cyclists and non-cyclists Since the research is exploratory in nature and deals with understanding behaviour, thoughts and experiences, qualitative data collection was preferred over quantitative data.

Where is this data? How do we acquire it?

The data can be acquired from articles, papers, books and from individuals. The data can be acquired from online platforms as well as offline.

What is the Method for collecting data?

Primary data - face to face interviews, engaging with people on social media platforms
Secondary data - published data, books, magazines, journals

Secondary data

Examples of Current Interventions to encourage cycling were identified and studied from all over the world. These innterventions could be categorized as:

  1. Cultural Acceptance: Which reflects in the policies by the government
  2. Technological Intervention: SocialCycle app, MoBike, Strava
  3. Social Initiatives: cycle2college, cycle2school
  4. Online social media groups

A swot analysis was conducted on the existing different cultural, social and technological interventions.

Primary data

Phase 1: Survey

The primary research was conducted in two phases. First phase or the pilot phase was to understand current scenario of the perception, usage and possible cycling opportunities for people.
The survey conducted with 37 participants (28 non-cyclists, 9 cyclists). Convenience sampling was done to select the participants for the primary research with the common factor being that all of them were residents of Mumbai.

Pro

Helped in identification of my types of users and narrow down on target audience

Con

Did not have access to a large sample. For such a project which needs to involve a large population, the survey conducted was extremely small and hence cannot be called statistically significant.

Phase 2: Interviews

After getting an overall idea as to what the current scenario and perception of cycling is of the probable target audience was, further interviews were conducted with 7 participants out of which 4 were cyclists (of age 25, 27, 32) and 3 were non-cyclists (23, 25, 26). The interview was conducted to understand their behavior, constraints and motivation.

Pro

Helped explain, better understand, and explore research subjects' opinions, behavior, experiences, phenomenon, etc. Interview questions are usually open-ended questions so that in-depth information will be collected.

Con

Did not have enough participants (For practical reasons Crouch & McKenzie (2006) propose that less than 20 participants in a qualitative study helps a researcher build and maintain a close relationship and thus improve the “open” and “frank” exchange of information.)

Insights

Behaviour

  1. Only 9 out of 37 participants own a cycle; out of them only 2 cycle daily
  2. 26 out of the other 28 owned a bicycle when they were kids
  3. To overcome the fear, people ride in groups
  4. People who used cycle for a non-recreational purpose in their childhood tend to use it in the future also

Motivation

  1. The primary motive of cycling is health
  2. Cycling lightens mood
  3. To overcome the fear, people ride in groups
  4. Nostalgia is attached to cycling

Constraints

  1. There is fear of riding bicycles in a mixed traffic
  2. Distance and time taken to reach the destination is an important factor while considering cycling for non-recreational purposes
  3. The behavior of non-cyclists is rude towards the cyclists
  4. Infrastructural facilities such as cycling tracks, proper, safe parking and shower at office are not available
  5. Cycling is not even perceived as a mode of trasportation
