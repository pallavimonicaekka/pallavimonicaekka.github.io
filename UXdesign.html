Project overview

Urban travel is one of the biggest concerns in cities and it mainly revolves around environmental factors and sustainability. Increase in the use of light weight vehicles does not only increase air pollution but also makes traveling on the roads time-consuming and inconvenient for the travelers along with the additional cost of the refueling and maintenance of the vehicle. Bicycles seem to be a cheaper and viable alternative to light weight vehicle and two-wheelers when it comes to covering short distances. Bicycles are cheap, Eco-friendly, healthy, faster in traffic and enjoyable when it comes to exploring places. Despite obvious benefits, seldom people prefer cycling.

Initial idea

The first primitive solution focused on community support and assistance.

From the primary research, it has been seen that people depend on the community since not much support is provided from the authoritative front. Community plays an important role in the initiation and well as providing continuous support to people willing to adopt cycling. People depend on others to overcome fear of the road traffic, to get assistance while traveling from one place to another, getting information about the road or traffic situation and also it plays an important part in providing and nurturing the motivation that is required to keep that spirit going.

The idea is to bring together people to help each other to succeed in the common aim that is to cycle for non-recreational purposes. The groups are made on the basis of geography since the travel on cycles for non-recreational purposes are shorter compared to recreational cycling. People when divided into groups according to the area they live would be able to provide each other with proper information and support in various ways necessary. The groups would be a mixture of enthusiastic cyclists as well as people who aren’t regular cyclists but wish to start cycling in near future.

Feedback

The idea was taken to 5 cyclists and they were taken through the scenario. The following was the feedback that was received from them. The feedback given by them was incorporated into the final concept.

  1. People are not curious enough explore cycling
  2. People generally don’t have any connection to cycling community
  3. There needs to be an intermediate activity - trial experience/ getting to know the cyclists in the locality better - before riding for a non-recreational purpose
  4. It was not clear as to how the platform would be discovered since the user is not involved in cycling at all
